Behaviors preferences

Use Behaviors preferences to configure Xcode to respond to certain events by performing one or more specific actions, such as playing a sound, displaying a notification, opening a tab, or running a script. Actions can be initiated when any of the following events occur:

  • A build starts, generates issues, succeeds, and fails.

  • Testing starts, pauses, generates issues, generates output, succeeds, and fails.

  • Running starts, pauses, generates output, completes, and exits unexpectedly.

  • GPU Frame Capture starts, completes, and finds issues.

  • A continuous integration bot succeeds, succeeds with warnings, fails, and generates an “I Caused Failure” alert.

  • Files are unlocked.

  • Playgrounds generate output.

To open Behaviors preferences, choose Xcode > Preferences and click Behaviors.
