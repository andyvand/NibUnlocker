Control movement and physics reactions

In the SpriteKit particle emitter editor, you can control different aspects of where a particle is created and the speed and angle at which the particle moves after creation.

Randomize the starting position

The Position Range fields define the area in which the emitter creates particles. Particles are created within the rectangle centered on the position defined in the scene and bounded by the position range values.

X and Y: These values create the vector corresponding to the particle emitter’s particlePositionRange property. The default value is (0.0,0.0).

Z position: The value corresponding to the particleZPosition property. The default value is 0.0.

  • Click the minus (—) or plus (+) button, or double-click in the field and enter a value.

Set the initial angle

The Angle fields control the direction, in degrees, that particles move away from the emitter. Entering 0 degrees moves the particles directly to the right. The degrees work on a counterclockwise rotation, so entering 90 in the Start field sends the particles to the top of the screen.

Tip: Degrees versus radians: The particle emitter inspector displays degrees when presenting an angle to the user. However, these degrees are converted into radians to match the SpriteKit API when the app is built.

Start: The average initial direction of a particle. This value corresponds to the particle emitter’s emissionAngle property.

Range: The range of allowed random values for particle’s direction at birth. This value corresponds to the particle emitter’s emissionAngleRange property.

  • Click the minus (—) or plus (+) button, or double-click in the field and enter the angle values.

Set a particle’s speed

The Speed fields define how fast particle is moving, in points per second, at the instance of creation.

The start and range values correspond to the particle emitter’s particleSpeed and particleSpeedRange properties.

  • Click the minus (—) or plus (+) buttons, or double-click in the fields and enter the speed values.

Change a particle’s acceleration after creation

The Acceleration fields modify the velocity of a particle after it is created. You can use this to simulate an overall gravity effect, wind blowing smoke from a fire, or other effects.

X: Specifies acceleration along the horizontal axis. This value corresponds to the particle emitter’s xAcceleration property.

Y: Specifies acceleration along the vertical axis. This value corresponds to the particle emitter’s yAcceleration property.

  • Click the minus (—) or plus (+) button, or double-click in the field and enter the acceleration values.

Enable particle reactions to physics fields

By default, particles are not affected by physics fields. Use the Field Mask menu to specify the categories of physics fields that can exert forces on your particles. The value you provide corresponds to the particle emitter’s fieldBitMask property.

  • Double-click in the field and enter a field mask value, or click the up or down arrow to change the value.
