What is localization?

Localization is the process of adapting your app to multiple languages. In App Store Connect, you can select territories where your app will be available on the App Store, as well as localize the App Store information. Then you localize your app in Xcode so those users experience your app in their own native language, region, and culture.

To prepare your app for localization, first add the languages you want to support to your project. Then mark the resources you want to vary for the languages as localizable. You can localize resources—such as user interface files, images, and audio files—for all or specific languages.

Then export localizations for the languages you are ready to support and give the language-specific Xcode Localization Catalog (xcloc) folders to localizers. Optionally, add files to the xcloc folder to provide context for localizers.

You can continue developing your app in the base language and, optionally, lock views while waiting for localization. Then you import localizations, the translated xcloc folders.

Thoroughly test your app in each language you support. Before you export localizations for translations, you can test localizations using pseudolanguages. After you import localizations, you can test your app in the languages and regions you added.
