Change the background and texture of a particle emitter

With the SpriteKit particle emitter editor, you can modify the look and feel of the particles created by the emitter. Changes made in the inspector take effect immediately and can be seen in the editor area.

Change the background color of an emitter

Using the Attributes inspector, you can change the background color shown in the Xcode preview to help you visualize how the emitter will look in different environments.

  • In the Background pop-up menu, choose a color.

    Tip: If the color you want to use is not listed, choose Other to bring up the color picker.

The color you pick for the background persists until you change it again. When the app is built, the background color is saved, but is not used at runtime. To create an emitter with no background color, set the opacity in the color picker to 0.

Choose the shape of a particle

Each particle created in the emitter is based on a texture image that can be a solid shape or a complex picture. You can use any image associated with the project as a particle texture. All the particle emitter’s modifications are applied to the selected image.

  • In the Texture pop-up field, choose a particle texture.

    Tip: Keep in mind that the larger or more complex a particle image is, the more resources it uses. Use the smallest and least complex image possible for the desired effect.
